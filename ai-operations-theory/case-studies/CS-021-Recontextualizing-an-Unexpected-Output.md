# CS-021 Recontextualizing an Unexpected Output

## Summary

This case documents how an unexpected AI-generated diagram, originally outside the intended task, was preserved and later adopted within a different editorial context.

Rather than treating the output solely as an instruction failure, it was evaluated against the publication's broader objectives and ultimately repurposed for another chapter.

The case illustrates how unexpected outputs can become editorial assets through human judgment and contextual reassignment.

---

## Background

During the development of a Rune Factory research publication, an AI assistant was asked to review an existing diagram.

The intention was not to generate a new figure, but to evaluate and improve the current one.

The original plan was to reuse the reviewed diagram across multiple chapters.

---

## Observation

Instead of reviewing the existing figure, the AI unexpectedly produced an entirely new diagram.

The newly generated figure did not satisfy the original Decision Context.

However, during editorial review, the diagram was recognized as providing a broader visual explanation than originally requested.

The original diagram remained well suited for the chapter explaining the mathematical model.

The newly generated diagram, however, was found to better support a later chapter discussing workload reduction in high-difficulty inheritance.

Rather than replacing the original figure, the publication adopted both diagrams, each within the context where it provided the greatest explanatory value.

---

## Decision Context

The objective was never to maximize AI-generated content.

The objective was to improve the publication.

Although the AI deviated from the original instruction, the resulting artifact was evaluated independently of that deviation.

Editorial judgment focused on a different question:

> Does this output contribute to the publication if placed within another context?

Once the answer became affirmative, the output was preserved and reassigned rather than discarded.

---

## Result

The original mathematical figure continued to serve its intended role.

The unexpected diagram became a dedicated visual explanation for a different chapter.

Instead of increasing inconsistency, the publication gained an additional explanatory layer without requiring an entirely new design effort.

An output that initially appeared to be an instruction deviation became an editorial asset through contextual reassignment.

---

## Working Observation

Unexpected AI outputs should not automatically be accepted.

Neither should they automatically be discarded.

When an output fails to satisfy its original Decision Context, it may still possess value under a different Decision Context.

The responsibility for identifying that alternative context remains with the human editor.

This observation is currently limited to the editorial process of the Rune Factory research publication.

Further observations are required before generalizing this operational pattern.
